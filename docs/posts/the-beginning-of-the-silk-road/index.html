The Beginning of the Silk Road

138 BCE: The Han armies marched westward as far as the Caspian Sea, pacifying nomadic peoples and extending China’s boundary far into Central Asia, originally to seek alliance to counter the common Xiongnu menace, but thereafter Chinese commercial exchanges with people in Central Asia began to expand dramatically.

Effects:

  • Spread of Buddhism from South Asia to China in the first century CE.
